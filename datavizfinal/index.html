Here we see patients' blood glucose (y axis) with respect to their age (x axis). Do you see the large gap on the right side of the graph? This interesting gap has a huge concentration of diseases right on its edges. Click "Show Diseases" to see...

The ejection fraction is a measure of the amount of blood the left ventricle pumps with each contraction of the heart. As we can see, a majority of patients have below optimal ejection fraction levels. As ejection fraction is not a commonly known measure, it is important for patients to learn ways to increase their ejection fraction levels, through diet, excercise, or other physician-recommended methods.
